School Survival Toolkit

Real School Tips from a Student

Dealing with Stress

Break the task down

A single overwhelming task or several of them can often feel daunting. Try to separate it into steps so it feels easier! For example, if you have this big project you're currently working on, try to work on one section of it first.

Take a breather

If everything feels too overwhelming right now, take just a second to relax and gather your thoughts. Listen to music, take a walk outside, etc. You can get through this, whether it's a particularly difficult homework problem or family troubles. You first need to take a second to get your thoughts in order

Talk to a trusted friend or adult

It might feel embarrassing, or even bad to talk about your difficulties with someone else, but sharing your struggles can really help. Whether it's a parent, teacher, counselor, or close friend, having someone listen and offer support can make a huge difference.

Studying Tips

Use the Pomodoro Technique

Work for 25-40 minutes, then take a 5-minute break.

Create a dedicated study space JUST for studying

Having a specific area just for studying can cause your brain to Focus.

Managing Social Situations

Quality over quantity

You should have a few good friends, instead of having many friends who you frequently do not speak to or spend time with.
